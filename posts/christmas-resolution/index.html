Christmas Resolution

December 18, 2021 · Harry Zhou

$\text{Feliz Navidad Amigos!}$

The long-awaited Christmas break has finally arrived. No need to wake up at 6:30 or finish the boring data structure homework anymore. YES. That being said, I also don’t want my break to be me pointlessly surfing the Internet and wasting time. This break is meant to be a time to rest, be productive and explore new interests. Therefore, I decided to create the following list of goals:

Top 5 Goals#

  1. Do 50 Codeforces problems with rating around 2200 and get back to Candidate Master.

My progress in competitive programming has been stagnant for the past year. One reason is that I can’t practice consistently. It seems almost as if my passion for CP has dwindled. Thus, the break is a perfect opportunity to get me back on track. 50 Problems translate to 3-4 problems a day, which is quite doable. There should be a few contests before break ends and I’ll be able to get back to Candidate Master if I am in my peak form.

  1. Finish 4-6 SUMaC Admission Problems. A good time to start the 2022 SUMaC admission problems.

  2. Finish 6 weeks of Machine Learning Course by Andrew Ng. I’ve always said that I am amazed by AI and Machine Learning, but I’ve never actually learnt how to do it. Now it’s the opportunity to explore new interests :)

  3. School work

    • Spanish presentation
    • Math HW
    • History reading

  4. Others

    • Dual boot my Windows laptop with Linux
    • Read Cinder, The Lunar Chronicles
    • Write blog posts
